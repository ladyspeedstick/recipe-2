Paneer is Indian cheese but it
doesn't melt.
So get that out of your head.

Why use it, then? Well because it's filling, it's a meat substitute and it gives the dish a more complex texture.
It also tastes a hell of a lot better than the average firm tofu.

Ingredients:

    Olive Oil
    Butter
    Cardamom
    Cumin
    Garam Masala
    Yellow or Red Mustard Seeds
    Onion
    Garlic
    Spinach
    Cilantro (optional)
    Lime Juice
    Almond or Peanut Butter
    Chicken (or veggie) Stock
    Tomato Paste
    Paneer

Important Notes:

  • Toasting the mustard seeds at the beginning makes you an honourary Indian. Totally adds legitimacy. Don't skip this step!
  • TOAST THOSE CURRIES!
    There's nothing worse than raw curry flavour,
    yuck.
  • There are no quantities for this recipe because curry isn't designed to be held back by quantities. I'll give you some approximates, but trust your palate!

Instructions:

  • Dice your paneer and sear each side in a frying pan with. Set aside.
  • Heat equal amounts of oil and butter in a dutch oven or stock pot that had a lid (enough to coat the bottom).
  • Toast your mustard seeds(maybe... 2 tbsp, 3-4 minutes).
  • Add your powdered curries/spices to taste (2 to 1 parts cumin to coriander, .5 part of cardamom, and as much of the garam masala or any other flexible Indian spice mix as you'd like!)
  • Roughly chop your onion and garlic (1/2 an onion roughly chopped and like, 4-6 cloves of garic is what I usually deal with). Soften in the delicious-smelling hot pot.
  • Add your spinch to the pot and wilt the crap out of it (put the lid on, simmer on LOW until you're all wilty).
  • Add your cilantro last to retain it's flavour. I put a whole bunch in, but this is optional.
  • Taking extreme care, put some of the contents of your stock pot in your blender and WHIZZ them up in batches! Blend that mess until it's consistancy is to your liking.
  • Add the blender contents back to the pot
  • To the green goop add the remaining ingredients to taste, adding the paneer last.
Saagcess!!
Serve with naan and rice, or just rice. If you make this the night before the flavors will have a chance to intermingle nicely. This dish makes excellent leftovers.
